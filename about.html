What Web-MMTL Is

MMTL was developed from Mayo Clinic transmission-line research beginning in the 1980s and was publicly released under the GPL in 2004. It is a 2-D/2.5-D field solver for trace impedance, propagation, crosstalk, and conductor coupling. TNT is the original GUI for Metal Everywhere; Web-MMTL is this browser interface.

It uses a boundary-element and method-of-moments field solution. It's as accurate as expensive commercial tools like Polar SI9000e.

The implementation is open source and inspectable.

Arbitrary Geometry and Dielectric Regions

Web-MMTL is much more accurate than formula-based impedance calculators. It models the cross-section that is actually being manufactured as a 2D field.

  • Mixed and stacked core, prepreg, resin, solder-mask, coating, and air-adjacent dielectric regions
  • A pure-resin region across, above, or beside a trace
  • Asymmetric stripline with different materials or distances above and below the conductor
  • Rectangular, etched-trapezoid, or circular conductors
  • Multiple signal and ground conductors with coupling and modal impedance results

Free-form Examples

This solver supports free-form geometries. These examples use that flexibility to calculate cable impedance from physical dimensions and dielectric properties.

Ribbon cable

The Belden 9R280 datasheet specifies 28 AWG, 7x36 tinned-copper conductors on 1.27 mm pitch in a 0.914 mm body. A ground-signal-ground group is nominally 105 ohm.

The model uses equivalent-area 0.336 mm conductors in a rounded 0.914 mm PVC body, with representative flexible PVC at er = 2.89 and estimated tan(delta) = 0.048. It predicts the specified value to within 0.5%.

Cat5e twisted pair

The Belden 1583ANS datasheet specifies 24 AWG solid copper, a 0.89 mm PE-insulated diameter, and 100 ohm nominal impedance.

The model uses 0.511 mm copper conductors inside rounded 0.89 mm PE sleeves at the insulated-diameter spacing, with representative PE at er = 2.34 and estimated tan(delta) = 0.00002. It predicts the specified value to within 4.8%.

JLCPCB Dielectric Support

This tool has JLCPCB-specific material support, including construction/thickness-dependent dielectric-constant references and frequency-dependent dielectric-loss models. JLCPCB currently uses Nan Ya NP-155F for 4-8-layer impedance calculations and Shengyi S1000-2M for 10-layer and higher constructions.
